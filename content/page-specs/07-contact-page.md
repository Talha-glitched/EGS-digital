# 07 Contact Page Content Spec

## Page Goal

Make it easy for serious buyers to brief EGS without filling a generic form. The page should gather the facts EGS needs to judge timing, scope, and feasibility.

## Audience

Primary: anyone ready to start a project conversation.  
Secondary: urgent-change buyers who need a fast feasibility read.

## SEO

Title: `Contact EGS Dubai | Brief Exhibition Stands, Events, Retail Rollouts`  
Meta: `Contact EGS in Dubai for exhibition stands, graduation ceremonies, retail branding rollouts, signage, and branded interiors. Send the deadline, location, and scope.`

## H1 And Hero

H1:

```text
Send the deadline, location, and what needs to happen.
```

Hero paragraph:

```text
The fastest way to start is to tell us the service, venue or location, deadline, and what has changed or needs to be ready. If the job is urgent, say that first.
```

Primary CTA: `Email EGS` -> `mailto:info@exhibitgraphicsign.com`  
Secondary CTA: `Call / WhatsApp EGS`

Visual direction: keep this page calm and practical. Use one real work image or small proof strip, but do not let visuals push contact details below the fold on desktop or mobile.

## Render Discipline

### Above The Fold

Must render:

- H1.
- Hero paragraph.
- Primary CTA: `Email EGS` or `Send us your brief`.
- Secondary CTA: `Call / WhatsApp EGS`.
- Direct contact details or contact buttons.
- Four brief-type cards should start near the first viewport.

Do not render above the fold:

- Long proof reminder.
- Full FAQ.
- Long form before contact buttons.
- Internal form guidance.

### Render As Visual, Not Text

- Choose Your Brief should be four clickable cards.
- What To Include should be a checklist.
- Recommended form fields should become actual form labels if a form is built.
- What Happens Next should be a 5-step strip.
- Direct contact should be a visible block with phone, WhatsApp, email, and location.
- Proof reminder should be a small strip with 3-4 proof chips.

### Show, Do Not Tell

- Do not say “get in touch” as the main idea. Show exactly what to send.
- Do not hide the phone/WhatsApp. Show it early.
- Do not make urgency abstract. Use proof chips: `33 locations overnight`, `10-hour stage extension`, `10-12 hour stand adaptation`.
- Do not force buyers to complete a long form before seeing direct contact options.

### Guidance Only, Do Not Render

- `If Talha adds a form...`
- `Optional field:`
- `Do not make budget mandatory...`
- `Use current confirmed site details...`
- `If any detail changes...`
- `What Not To Say`
- `Implementation Notes`

## Section Order

### 1. Hero

Must be practical and direct.

### 2. Choose Your Brief

Cards:

- Brief us on a stand  
  `Send show name, stand size, location, deadline, and product/display needs.`
- Brief us on a ceremony  
  `Send event date, venue, guest/graduation scale, stage needs, and what must be ready.`
- Send a retail rollout list  
  `Send number of locations, access windows, launch date, asset types, and installation scope.`
- Ask about a fitout  
  `Send space type, location, handover target, brand/signage needs, and photos or drawings.`

### 3. What To Include

Checklist:

- service type,
- company name,
- contact name,
- phone/email,
- venue/show/location,
- deadline,
- current stage of project,
- what changed or what needs to happen,
- drawings, photos, location list, or brand guidelines if available.

### 4. Recommended Form Fields

If Talha adds a form, keep it short enough that urgent buyers still complete it.

Fields:

- `Name`
- `Company`
- `Phone / WhatsApp`
- `Email`
- `Service type`
- `Venue, show, mall, campus, or location`
- `Deadline / opening date / event date`
- `What needs to happen`
- `Upload brief, drawings, photos, location list, or brand files`

Optional field:

- `Has anything changed late?`

Do not make budget mandatory unless the sales team specifically wants it.

### 5. What Happens Next

Heading:

```text
What EGS checks first.
```

Copy:

```text
EGS looks at the date, location, access, scope, materials, and what needs to move first. For urgent work, the first reply should focus on feasibility and the next practical step.
```

Steps:

- `Review the brief`
- `Check deadline and access`
- `Confirm missing information`
- `Share feasibility or next questions`
- `Move into quote, site visit, design, or production planning`

### 7. Proof Reminder

Heading:

```text
If it is urgent, lead with the deadline.
```

Copy:

```text
EGS has handled 33-location overnight retail installation, 10-hour ceremony stage changes, 10-12 hour exhibition stand adaptation, and last-minute pavilion product display changes. The earlier we know the hard constraint, the faster we can judge what is possible.
```

### 8. FAQ

- `What should I send first?`  
  `Send the service type, deadline, venue or location, and the main outcome. If something has changed late, say that first.`
- `Can EGS respond to urgent requests?`  
  `Yes, when the work is physically possible and the standard can be protected. Send the deadline and scope so EGS can assess feasibility.`
- `Do I need a full brief?`  
  `No. A location, deadline, photos/drawings, and what needs to be ready are enough to start the conversation.`
- `Which services can I ask about?`  
  `Exhibition stands, graduation ceremonies, event production, retail branding, signage, and branded interiors.`
- `Will EGS tell me if the deadline is not realistic?`  
  `Yes. EGS checks feasibility first: deadline, access, material availability, scope, and what needs to move before production starts.`

## What Not To Say

- Do not make this a vague "get in touch" page only.
- Do not hide phone/email behind too much form copy.
- Do not ask for unnecessary fields before the user can contact EGS.
- Do not force clever CTA language. Contact CTAs should be direct.

## Implementation Notes

- This is a new `/contact` route.
- If a form is added, keep email/phone/WhatsApp visible outside the form.
- Use a practical layout: brief cards, checklist, contact block, proof reminder.
- On mobile, direct contact buttons should appear early and remain easy to reach.
