## Render Discipline

### Above The Fold

Must render:

- H1.
- Hero paragraph.
- Primary CTA: `Brief us on your ceremony`.
- Secondary CTA: `Read HCT proof`.
- HCT ceremony image.
- One visible stat cluster: `7 ceremonies`, `4,500 graduates`, `13,500 guests`.

Do not render above the fold:

- Public proof nuance.
- Full process.
- Full FAQ.
- Internal instruction phrases like `Use full ceremony production scope unless...`.

### Render As Visual, Not Text

- HCT proof should be large stats, not a paragraph-heavy block.
- Fujairah proof should be a compact story card with `10 hours`, `5-6 metre extension`, `Zayed Sports Complex`.
- Event process should be a stepper working backwards from showtime.
- Buyer reassurance should be small cards for ceremony leads, leadership, event managers, procurement, and guests.
- Public proof note should be a small caption or source note near the HCT case link.

### Show, Do Not Tell

- Do not say “high-stakes events” without showing HCT scale.
- Do not say “urgent changes” without showing the Fujairah 10-hour stage extension.
- Do not say “ceremony dignity” in a long paragraph. Show audience, stage, VIP/guest readiness, and fixed showtime.

### Guidance Only, Do Not Render

- `Use "full ceremony production scope" unless...`
- `Keep this section short.`
- `Do not invent non-graduation event case studies.`
- `Must say:`
- `What Not To Say`
- `Implementation Notes`
- Any warning about public articles unless rewritten as a short public source note.

### 5. Event Process

Heading:

```text
The plan works backwards from showtime.
```

Steps:

- `Send the event facts`: date, venue, audience size, ceremony type, stage needs, branding, and what must be ready.
- `Check the room`: EGS reviews access, stage position, guest flow, production constraints, and deadline risk.
- `Agree the scope`: stage, branding, display areas, production environment, installation timing, and handover responsibilities.
- `Prepare and source`: materials, graphics, carpentry, production items, and crew planning are lined up before site work.
- `Install and adapt`: the room is built around venue access and any late physical changes.
- `Hand over before doors open`: final checks happen before guests, graduates, VIPs, or leadership enter.
