- What EGS builds should be a compact capability grid.

- Build process should be a 5-step strip.

- Do not write “we handle pavilion needs” alone. Show Kazakhstan Pavilion at Gulfood 2026 with 5-6 added branded chillers.
- Do not write “we understand exhibition pressure” alone. Show opening day, venue access, product display, stand size, and time window.

### 4. What Buyers Need Reassured

Heading:

```text
The stand has to satisfy marketing, sales, procurement, and the venue.
```

Cards:

- `Marketing`: the stand should match the brand and photograph well.
- `Sales teams`: demos, counters, displays, storage, and movement need to make sense on the floor.
- `Procurement`: scope, deadline, and changes need clear handling.
- `Regional teams`: brand standards have to survive local production and build-up pressure.
- `Venue reality`: access windows, install rules, and late changes have to be managed on site.

### 5. Exhibition Build Process

Heading:

```text
What happens after you brief EGS.
```

Steps:

- `Send the stand details`: show name, hall, stand size, open sides, deadline, floorplan, products, and brand files.
- `Check feasibility`: EGS reviews timing, venue access, materials, displays, and any late risks.
- `Agree the build direction`: design intent, production scope, graphics, counters, demo areas, and installation plan.
- `Produce and prepare`: fabrication, printing, sourcing, and team planning move before build-up.
- `Install and finish`: the stand is built, adapted where needed, checked, and handed over before opening.

Keep this section practical. It should reassure buyers without becoming a manual.
