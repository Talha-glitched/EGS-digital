## Render Discipline

### Above The Fold

Must render:

- H1.
- Hero paragraph.
- Primary CTA: `Start a fitout brief`.
- Secondary CTA: `See relevant work`.
- Strong fitout/showroom/signage image if available.
- One short capability line: `Branded interiors, signage, joinery, and handover-ready spaces.`

Do not render above the fold:

- Long philosophy about fitouts.
- Proof caveats.
- Internal notes about weak proof.
- Full process.

### Render As Visual, Not Text

- What EGS builds should be a clean capability grid with photos where possible.
- Fitout process should be a 5-step strip.
- Buyer reassurance should be cards focused on Operations, Brand Teams, Property/Showroom Teams, Procurement, and Owners.
- Proof/credibility should be a small client/image strip unless detailed scope is confirmed.
- Use material/detail captions instead of broad claims.

### Show, Do Not Tell

- Do not say “good design” without showing signage, joinery, reception, display, or branded surface details.
- Do not say “handover quality” without showing a finished space or close-up detail.
- Do not over-explain fitout strategy. Show the room, surface, sign, display, and daily-use logic.

### 3. What EGS Builds

Cards:

- branded showrooms,
- reception and office branding,
- interior signage,
- joinery details,
- hospitality/retail-adjacent interiors,
- handover-ready branded spaces.

### 4. Fitout Process

Heading:

```text
The room has to work before it can look finished.
```

Steps:

- `Share the space`: send photos, drawings, location, handover target, and brand/signage needs.
- `Check daily use`: EGS reviews movement, visibility, signage placement, surfaces, storage, and wear points.
- `Agree the scope`: branded interiors, signage, joinery, graphics, display details, and handover expectations.
- `Produce`: fabrication, joinery, printing, signage, and finishing are prepared around the agreed scope.
- `Install and snag`: the work is installed, checked, adjusted, and handed over for daily use.

### 5. What Buyers Need Reassured

Heading:

```text
The space has to survive normal days.
```

Cards:

- `Operations`: people should move through the room without confusion.
- `Brand teams`: signage and brand details should feel built into the space.
- `Property/showroom teams`: finish quality matters after handover, not only in photos.
- `Procurement`: scope should stay practical and inspectable.
- `Owners/founders`: the space should feel credible to visitors and usable for staff.

### 6. How EGS Thinks About Fitouts

Bullets:

- `Brand visibility should be built into the space, not added at the end.`
- `Joinery and signage have to survive daily use.`
- `The room has to guide people without needing explanation.`
- `Handover should leave fewer questions, not more.`
